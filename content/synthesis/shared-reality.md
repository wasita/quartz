# shared reality

- perceived commonality of inner states (feelings, beliefs, concerns) with another person about something [@higgins2021shared]
- dual-motive construct
    1. relational — need to connect
    2. epistemic — need to understand the world

    - not independent of each other; synergistic
    - rejects treating cognition and connection as separable
    - sharing inner states with others simultaneously makes those states feel more objectively true _and_ deepens social bonds

## defining features

- four criteria that distinguish shared reality from related constructs [@echterhoff2009shared]:
    1. **subjective commonality** — must be _experienced_ as shared, not merely objectively overlapping
    2. **specific referent** — about something in the world (a target, event, situation)
    3. **motivational engagement** — requires active motivation to connect, not passive co-occurrence
    4. **experienced connection** — must involve felt epistemic or relational connection to the other person

## why it matters

- humans have a fundamental need to belong — a drive to form and maintain lasting positive relationships that shapes emotion, cognition, behavior, and health across cultures [@baumeister1995need]
- loneliness and social isolation independently predict premature mortality with effect sizes comparable to smoking and obesity [@holtlunstad2015loneliness]
- poor social relationships associated with ~29% increased CHD risk and ~32% increased stroke risk [@valtorta2016loneliness]
- shared reality is a core mechanism through which social bonds are formed and sustained

# how shared reality is created

## audience-tuning / sharing-is-believing

- when people communicate about a target to an audience, they tune their message to fit the audience's attitude [@echterhoff2018shared]
    - this tuning, crucially, shapes their own memory of the target
    - memory distortion only occurs when communicator is genuinely motivated to establish shared reality with audience
- shared reality created about one target can generalize to novel related targets — under conditions of high audience commonality, active recollection, and target similarity [@bebermeier2015generalization]

## shared experience and coattention

- sharing an experience with another person — even without explicit communication — amplifies its subjective intensity [@boothby2014shared]
    - both pleasant and unpleasant sensations rated more intensely in shared conditions
    - mechanism: coattention recruits greater attentional and cognitive resources
- collective attention functions as a signal of common knowledge and amplifies cognitive processing [@shteynberg2020shared]
- however, non-communicative online co-presence (e.g., passively co-watching video) fails to activate bonding mechanisms — equivalence tests showed no difference from solo-watching [@wolf2025online]
- in-person co-watching with multimodal synchrony (facial expressions, EDA, character impressions) does predict social connection — these indicators load onto a latent shared-experience factor [@cheong2023synchronized]

## self-disclosure

- intimate disclosers are liked more, people disclose more to liked others, and disclosing increases liking for the recipient — disclosure and liking form a dynamic, mutually reinforcing system [@collins1994self]
- listeners report greater liking and closeness after one-way disclosure, but differences disappear after role reversal; perceived similarity partially mediates the listener advantage [@sprecher2013effects]

## implicit social tuning

- affiliative social tuning: spontaneous alignment of implicit attitudes (measured via IAT) with an interaction partner's apparent views [@skorinko2018shared]
    - implicit prejudice shifts to mirror partners' presumed attitudes when affiliative motivation is high
    - shared reality can form at levels below conscious awareness

## behavioral signals

- conversational response time is a simple, robust, and honest signal of social connection — faster inter-turn response times (particularly sub-250ms) predict greater felt connection and are readable by both participants and eavesdroppers [@templeton2022fast]

# generalized shared reality (SR-G)

- measured using [self-report questionnaire](https://cuhigginslab.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/Generalized-Shared-Reality-SR-G-Rossignac-Milon.pdf)
- @rossignac2021merged
- sense of sharing inner states with a partner about the world _in general_
- theoretically distinct from:
    - target-specific shared reality
    - perceived similarity
    - partner responsiveness
- predicts connection, rapport, epistemic uncertainty, desire to interact again, above and beyond all 3 above ^

# epistemic companionship

- close relationships are epistemic partnerships — partners jointly construct shared reality [@rossignac2018epistemic]
- shared reality in relationships progresses through four phases:
    1. shared feelings
    2. shared practices
    3. shared coordination
    4. shared identity
- partners become "epistemic companions" who collaboratively make sense of the world

# similarity, projection, and the self-as-anchor

- homophily: contact between similar people occurs at higher rates, confining social networks to similar others and shaping opportunities, health, and political views [@mcpherson2001birds]
- attraction is a positive linear function of the proportion of similar attitudes [@byrne1965attraction]
- shared music preferences create interpersonal bonds through signaling value similarity (not personality similarity) — perceived value similarity mediates the link [@boer2011shared]
- music is the most frequent topic in get-acquainted conversations and carries valid personality information [@rentfrow2006message]

## cognitive mechanisms of person modeling

- people routinely use their own knowledge as a default anchor when constructing models of what others know, leading to systematic overestimation of shared knowledge — this unifies false consensus, hindsight bias, and curse of knowledge under knowledge imputation with insufficient adjustment [@nickerson1999knowledge]
- the false consensus effect: tendency to overestimate how widely others share one's own beliefs and behaviors [@ross1977false]
- perceived similarity acts as a switch: people use projection (self as model) for similar targets and stereotyping (group membership) for dissimilar targets [@ames2004projection]
- social inference follows anchoring-and-adjustment from the self — greater self-other discrepancy predicts longer reaction times, consistent with serial adjustment from a self-anchor [@tamir2013anchoring]
- speakers show egocentric bias toward their own knowledge when formulating messages; message informativeness varies inversely with perceived addressee familiarity [@fussell1992coordination]

# communication and common ground

- communication depends on common ground; communities are the primary source [@clark1996communities]
    - language is a coordination device grounded in community-level shared knowledge
    - both communal (from community membership) and personal (from direct interaction) common ground are necessary
- common ground as complex propositional attitudes built iteratively from individual attitudes — the context of conversation is the set of propositions all participants mutually accept [@stalnaker2002common]
- CHAI model: meaning is jointly inferred across two nested timescales — partner-specific common ground and community-wide conventions [@hawkins2023partners]
    - reconciles stable linguistic conventions with flexible partner-specific meaning-making

# scope: local vs. global shared reality

- psychological distance determines whether people align with local (specific individuals) or global (broader group norms) social influences [@ledgerwood2018achieving]
    - proximity → alignment with specific individuals
    - distance → alignment with group norms
- shared reality in politics: the desire to share reality is a motivational engine behind political attitude adoption and sustains conflicts through competing group-level representations [@stern2018political]

# developmental origins

- two-year-olds already identify shared preferences and use them to infer novel object choices — though generalization is category-specific [@fawcett2010children]

# computational approaches to social cognition

- BToM (bayesian theory of mind): models joint inference of beliefs, desires, and percepts from observed behavior — accurately captures quantitative human mental-state judgments [@baker2017rational]
- emotion prediction as computation over a generative theory of mind: observers infer mental states via inverse planning, compute appraisals, then map to emotions; social preferences are essential and irreducible [@houlihan2023emotion]
- social-structure learning: people infer latent group membership from observed behavior via bayesian inference, going beyond dyadic similarity [@gershman2020social; @gershman2017learning]
- person knowledge acquisition integrates face/voice recognition with person-specific trait and mental-state inference through cascading representations [@anzellotti2020acquisition]

# key paradigm shifts

- psychology has been limited by focus on isolated individuals; the most important determinants of development, cognition, and mental health emerge from social interaction — a science of interacting minds requires new paradigms and tools [@wheatley2024emerging]

# open questions

- how does shared reality interact with computational models of social cognition? can bayesian models of theory of mind (e.g., BToM) be extended to capture the _creation_ of shared reality through iterative communication?
- what role does recursive mentalizing play in establishing shared reality (i.e., the process of establishing shared reality through conversation)?
- what distinguishes shared reality from mere common knowledge or common ground computationally?
- how does the self-as-anchor bias interact with shared reality formation — does anchoring on one's own states facilitate or hinder genuine shared reality?
- can the generalization of shared reality (from one target to novel targets) be modeled as hierarchical bayesian inference?
