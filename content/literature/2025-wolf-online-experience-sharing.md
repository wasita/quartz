# summary

>[!example] key terms
> social bonding, shared experience, online interaction, video-mediated communication, screen sharing, equivalence testing, social presence

wolf and dotson tested whether passively co-watching a video online without communication fosters social bonding. equivalence tests showed no difference between co-watching and solo-watching, suggesting non-communicative online co-presence fails to activate bonding mechanisms.

full citation: [@wolf2025]

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

whether digital shared experiences produce the same bonding as in-person ones is important for remote work, isolation, and social technology design.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

shared intentionality accounts (tomasello) posit bonding requires joint attention and mutual awareness. social presence theory predicts reduced bonding in mediated contexts.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

in-person studies showed non-communicative co-presence enhances bonding. no pre-registered study had tested whether this transfers online.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

fundamental bonding mechanisms from in-person shared experiences may not transfer to non-communicative online contexts due to insufficient shared intentionality.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

pre-registered equivalence hypothesis: social bonding will not differ between co-watching and solo-watching conditions.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

pre-registered between-subjects experiment; n=144; participants watched video on shared screen with pre-recorded partners or individually; no communication permitted.

> ***what data is used?***

self-reported social bonding measures; n=144.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

equivalence testing (TOST); between-subjects design with random assignment.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

equivalence tests confirmed bonding was practically equivalent between conditions. no significant difference from online co-presence.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

passive online co-presence does not replicate in-person bonding, consistent with shared intentionality accounts.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

non-communicative online experience sharing does not foster social bonding. explicit communication or richer interaction may be necessary.
