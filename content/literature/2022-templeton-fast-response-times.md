# summary

>[!example] key terms
> social connection, response time, conversation, turn-taking, interpersonal coordination, honest signaling, rapport

templeton et al. demonstrate that conversational response time is a simple, robust, and honest signal of social connection. in round-robin studies, faster inter-turn response times predicted greater felt connection, and sub-250ms responses served as honest signals readable by both participants and eavesdroppers.

full citation: [@templeton2022]

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

how do people signal and detect social connection in real time? identifying automatic cues to rapport has implications for relationship formation and social technologies.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

conversational coordination theories predict aligned timing reflects shared engagement. pragmatic theories of turn-taking treat response latency as socially meaningful. honest signaling frameworks predict hard-to-fake cues are more reliable.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

response latencies vary with social context and relational quality. whether latency independently signals connection beyond other cues had not been directly tested.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

fast response times are not merely correlated with connection but constitute a sufficient, honest signal. sub-250ms responses preclude conscious fabrication and reliably index felt connection.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

(1) faster response times predict more connection; (2) within conversations, faster moments coincide with greater connection; (3) effect driven by partner responsiveness; (4) eavesdroppers use response time to judge connection.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

round-robin design: 66 undergraduates completed 10-minute conversations with each of 10 group members, yielding 322 dyads.

> ***what data is used?***

audio recordings with automated turn-detection; self-reported connection ratings; third-party observer ratings.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

multilevel modeling; actor-partner interdependence model; separate eavesdropper study.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

faster response times predicted higher connection; effect driven by partner speed; sub-250ms responses indexed connection; eavesdroppers accurately used response time to judge rapport.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

all four hypotheses supported. response latency is a causal signal of connection.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

conversational response time is a simple, honest signal of social connection operating below conscious control.
