# summary

>[!example] key terms
> self-disclosure, liking, intimacy, meta-analysis, reciprocity, relationship development, interpersonal attraction

collins and miller meta-analyze 94 studies and identify three distinct disclosure-liking effects: intimate disclosers are liked more, people disclose more to liked others, and disclosing increases liking for the recipient.

full citation: [@collins1994]

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

self-disclosure is a primary mechanism of relationship development. clarifying the disclosure-liking link is essential for understanding how relationships form.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

social penetration theory predicts gradual disclosure deepens intimacy. uncertainty reduction theory predicts disclosure increases liking. the paper integrates these by identifying three distinct effects forming a dynamic system.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

prior studies were inconsistent because they conflated three distinct causal pathways. this meta-analysis is the first systematic integration.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

disclosure-liking is a three-part dynamic system: (a) disclosers are liked more, (b) liking promotes disclosure, (c) disclosing generates liking toward the recipient.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

(1) intimate disclosers liked more. (2) people disclose more to liked others. (3) disclosing increases liking for the recipient. moderators: gender, disclosure type, paradigm.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

meta-analysis of 94 published and unpublished studies.

> ***what data is used?***

effect sizes from 94 empirical studies.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

meta-analytic integration; moderator analyses using paradigm, disclosure type, and gender.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

all three effects significant and moderate in size. intimate disclosure produced more liking. gender moderated some effects.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

all hypotheses confirmed; disclosure and liking are mutually reinforcing.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

disclosure and liking operate as a dynamic system with three mutually reinforcing pathways.
