# summary

>[!example] key terms
> person knowledge, social perception, face recognition, person identity, social cognition, neural mechanisms, trait inference

anzellotti and young review how people acquire knowledge about specific individuals, integrating face/voice recognition with person-specific trait and mental-state inference. person knowledge is built through cascading representations linking perceptual individuation to social and semantic knowledge.

full citation: [@anzellotti2020acquisition]

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

knowing specific individuals is foundational to social life, yet most social cognition research focuses on category-based processing. understanding individual-level person knowledge fills a major gap.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

bruce and young's face recognition model, haxby et al.'s distributed neural model, theory of mind, and predictive coding frameworks. a gap exists between perceptual identity recognition and higher-level social inference.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

neuroimaging shows person-specific knowledge in anterior temporal lobe and mPFC. familiarity enhances mentalizing accuracy. evidence on dynamic updating of person models is sparse.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

person knowledge requires coordinated processes across perceptual individuation, action recognition, and social inference. these levels interact - prior knowledge shapes perception and inference top-down.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

review paper. key claims: (1) cascading representations; (2) familiarity modulates perception and inference; (3) distributed neural representations.
