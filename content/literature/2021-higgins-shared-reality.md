# summary

>[!example] key terms
> shared reality, inner states, social cognition, motivation, interpersonal relationships

Higgins presents a comprehensive theory of shared reality — the experience of having in common with others' inner states about the world. he argues that shared reality is a fundamental human motivation that shapes cognition, emotion, and social behavior.

full citation: [@higgins2021shared]

---

# topic and thesis

shared reality is proposed as a basic human need: people are motivated to establish shared inner states with others. this motivation underlies social influence, group identity, and interpersonal connection — and when it breaks down, it contributes to polarization and conflict.

---

# background

the book integrates decades of research on social tuning, audience design, the "saying is believing" effect, and related phenomena under a unifying framework of shared reality theory.

---

# argument and hypotheses

Higgins argues that shared reality is not merely agreement on facts but the experience of sharing inner states (beliefs, feelings, evaluations) about the world. it requires both a perceived commonality and a motivation to establish that commonality with specific others.

---

# conclusion

shared reality is both a source of human strength (enabling cooperation, trust, and shared meaning) and a source of division (when in-group shared realities conflict with out-group perspectives).
