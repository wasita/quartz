# summary

>[!example] key terms
> social influence, latent structure learning, bayesian model, group inference, social learning, choice imitation, cultural transmission

gershman et al. propose that humans infer latent group structure of social influence - clustering based on shared choice patterns - rather than relying on dyadic similarity alone. a bayesian latent-grouping model is validated across five experiments.

full citation: [@gershman2017learning]

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

social influence is pervasive but the mechanisms for deciding whose choices to follow are not fully understood. latent group structure as a mechanism has implications for cultural transmission and conformity.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

dyadic similarity models, cultural group selection, and bayesian social learning. the novel contribution is formalizing structured group inference as the basis of social influence.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

people are more influenced by similar others. asch documented group size and unanimity effects. no unified account existed for both dyadic and group effects.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

social influence is governed by inferred latent group structure: people cluster themselves and others based on choice patterns and follow inferred in-group members.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

the latent-group model will explain patterns beyond dyadic similarity; explicit groupings will match model predictions; latent grouping can shift existing preferences.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

five behavioral experiments using choice-observation paradigms.

> ***what data is used?***

behavioral choice data across five experiments; experiment 4 included explicit grouping judgments.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

bayesian latent-structure model (nonparametric clustering) compared against dyadic similarity models.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

experiments 2-3: latent-group model uniquely predicted influence when dyadic overlap was equated. experiment 4: explicit groupings matched model. experiment 5: latent grouping altered preferences.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

social influence operates via group-level inference, not only pairwise similarity.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

humans infer latent social group structure from choice patterns and use it to guide social influence.
