# summary

>[!example] key terms
> social-structure learning, bayesian inference, group identity, intergroup cognition, stereotype updating, coalition formation, latent group structure

gershman and cikara formalize social-structure learning - inferring latent group membership from observed behavior - as a bayesian inference problem. experiments with adults and children support the framework and rule out simpler dyadic similarity accounts.

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

how group structure is learned from experience has broad implications for stereotype formation and intergroup bias. the computational mechanisms were poorly understood.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

structure learning (kemp & tenenbaum), social identity theory, categorization theories. prior accounts lacked a formal computational treatment.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

evidence on categorical perception, ingroup-outgroup biases, and developmental group reasoning. prior work rarely distinguished latent-structure learning from dyadic similarity.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

social group structure is inferred as a latent variable; people combine individual behavior information with prior beliefs about group structure, consistent with bayesian non-parametric approaches.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

group assignments will be consistent with bayesian latent-structure inference, not simple dyadic similarity; this will generalize to children.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

theoretical framework paper with behavioral experiments (adults and children).

> ***what data is used?***

behavioral data from group assignment tasks; developmental data from children.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

bayesian computational modeling; behavioral experiments; model comparison with dyadic-similarity alternatives.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

adults and children assign individuals to groups consistent with bayesian latent-structure inference, beyond dyadic similarity.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

social-structure learning is a genuine inferential process over latent group variables.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

social-structure learning is a bayesian inference problem operating over latent group structure, enabling flexible group-based reasoning.
