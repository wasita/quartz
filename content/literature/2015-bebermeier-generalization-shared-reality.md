# summary

>[!example] key terms
> shared reality, message tuning, generalization, audience commonality, attitude change, social communication, evaluative consistency

bebermeier et al. show that shared reality created about one target generalizes to evaluations of novel related targets, but only under high audience commonality, active recollection, and target similarity.

full citation: [@bebermeier2015generalization]

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

whether shared reality effects extend beyond the specific discussed target matters for understanding how shared perspectives propagate.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

shared reality theory (echterhoff et al., 2009), saying-is-believing effect, and cognitive consistency theories.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

the saying-is-believing effect was established for specific targets. whether it extends to new targets was unknown.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

shared reality is not target-specific but can generalize when conditions for recollection and perceived commonality are met. shared understanding acts as a lens for evaluating similar new targets.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

(1) generalization under high audience commonality. (2) persistence only with recall of initial shared reality. (3) no generalization for dissimilar/unambiguous targets.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

two laboratory experiments using message-tuning paradigm; experiment 1 manipulated commonality; experiment 2 added one-week delay and recall manipulation.

> ***what data is used?***

written communication tasks; evaluative ratings of new ambiguous targets.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

within-subjects message tuning; bogus feedback for commonality manipulation; one-week follow-up; mediation/moderation analyses.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

experiment 1: generalization under high commonality. experiment 2: generalization persisted at one week only with recall; no generalization for non-similar targets.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

generalization is conditional: commonality provides motivation, recollection provides the mechanism, target similarity sets the boundary.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

shared reality about one target can generalize to related targets under specific conditions, extending shared reality theory.
