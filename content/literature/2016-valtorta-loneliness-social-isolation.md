# summary

>[!example] key terms
> loneliness, social isolation, coronary heart disease, stroke, cardiovascular disease, systematic review, longitudinal studies

valtorta et al. meta-analyzed longitudinal studies showing approximately 29% increased CHD risk and 32% increased stroke risk associated with poor social relationships, comparable to anxiety and job strain.

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

CHD and stroke are leading causes of death. identifying modifiable psychosocial risk factors informs prevention strategies.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

psychosocial stress models link isolation to HPA axis dysregulation and inflammation. loneliness is situated alongside anxiety, depression, and job strain as a risk factor.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

prior meta-analyses established links to all-cause mortality but not specific cardiovascular endpoints. measurement heterogeneity is a known challenge.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

loneliness and social isolation are independent psychosocial risk factors for CHD and stroke, warranting inclusion in cardiovascular prevention frameworks.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

poor social relationships will predict increased CHD and stroke incidence after adjustment for established risk factors.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

systematic review and meta-analysis following PRISMA guidelines.

> ***what data is used?***

23 papers from 16 longitudinal datasets; 4,628 CHD events; 3,002 stroke events; follow-up 3-21 years.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

systematic database search; quality assessment; random-effects meta-analysis; pooled relative risks.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

CHD: RR = 1.29 (95% CI 1.04-1.59). stroke: RR = 1.32 (95% CI 1.04-1.68). effect sizes comparable to anxiety and job strain.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

social relationship deficits are a clinically relevant cardiovascular risk factor.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

loneliness and social isolation are associated with significantly elevated cardiovascular risk. interventions targeting these factors should be tested.
