# summary

>[!example] key terms
> social connection, affect synchrony, shared experience, facial expression synchrony, electrodermal activity, interpersonal alignment, naturalistic paradigm

cheong et al. measured facial expression synchrony, EDA synchrony, and character impression similarity in dyads co-watching TV. all four indicators loaded onto a latent shared-experience factor that predicted self-reported social connection.

full citation: [@cheong2023]

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

understanding the mechanisms by which shared experiences generate social bonds is fundamental. identifying affective and physiological mediators has implications for theory and social environment design.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

interpersonal synchrony theories posit alignment promotes affiliation. shared reality theory argues cognitive and affective alignment produces felt connection. prior work was limited to single modalities.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

dyadic behavioral synchrony predicts rapport; physiological synchrony associated with cooperation; facial mirroring linked to bonding. no study had jointly examined multiple synchrony modalities in a naturalistic paradigm.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

shared affective experiences produce social connection through multiple simultaneous channels - facial, physiological, and cognitive - best represented as a latent shared-experience factor.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

(1) co-watching dyads show greater synchrony. (2) synchrony predicts social connection. (3) a latent factor comprising all indicators accounts for the relationship.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

naturalistic longitudinal experiment; 86 participants watched four episodes of friday night lights alone or in dyads.

> ***what data is used?***

continuous facial expressions, EDA, character impression ratings, and self-reported connection; n=86.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

automated facial action unit coding; EDA cross-correlation; impression similarity; latent variable analysis; multilevel regression.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

dyads showed greater facial synchrony. all four indicators predicted connection. a latent shared-experience factor predicted connection above individual indicators.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

shared experiences generate connection through coordinated multi-modal responses, not any single channel.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

synchronized affect across facial, physiological, and cognitive channels during shared experiences strengthens social connection.
