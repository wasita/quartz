# summary

>[!example] key terms
> shared reality, psychological distance, construal level theory, social influence, group norms, attitude alignment, abstract construal

ledgerwood and wang review how psychological distance from an attitude object determines whether people align with local, specific social influences or broader group-level norms in the service of achieving shared reality.

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

people receive conflicting cues from specific individuals and general group norms. understanding when they tune to each type of influence matters for attitude formation and polarization.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

integrates construal level theory (trope & liberman) with shared reality theory (echterhoff, higgins & levine, 2009). distance governs which social referents become relevant.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

distance increases abstract construal and reliance on general principles. shared reality research showed people tune to audiences. the conditions governing specific vs. general influence were not integrated.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

psychological distance guides which social influence people seek: proximity prompts alignment with specific individuals (local shared reality), distance prompts alignment with group norms (global shared reality).

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

near objects prompt alignment with specific partners; distant objects prompt alignment with group norms.

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

when evaluating distal objects, people rely more on group opinion and less on specific partners'. distant-future policy attitudes tracked group norms more.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

the same object can prompt different social alignment depending on psychological distance, supporting the CLT-shared reality integration.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

psychological distance regulates which social sources people draw on, shifting from interpersonal to normative alignment as distance increases.
