# summary

>[!example] key terms
> lexical uncertainty, ad hoc coordination, communication, color-concept associations, common ground, pragmatics, convention formation

murthy et al. show that variability in lexical priors shapes communication difficulty and success. individuals represent their own uncertainty and use it to predict partner alignment; initial failures from high uncertainty are rapidly overcome through ad hoc convention formation.

---

# topic and thesis

> ***briefly, why is the research question important to answer?***

communication requires shared conventions but partners bring different expectations. understanding how lexical uncertainty is detected, represented, and resolved is fundamental to pragmatics.

---

# background

> ***what are the main theories in the literature? is there anything missing or problematic?***

probabilistic theories of pragmatics (RSA), common ground theory (clark), convention formation (lewis), and bayesian models of lexical meaning. prior work rarely examined how uncertainty about conventions is represented predictively.

> ***what is the empirical evidence? is there anything missing or problematic?***

prior work on color naming, ad hoc convention formation, and RSA models. the role of individual uncertainty representations in predicting communication success was untested.

---

# argument and hypotheses

> ***does the article advance a new theoretical argument? if yes, what is it?***

lexical priors are probabilistic; within-individual variability tracks between-individual variability and predicts communication difficulty. when priors misalign, interlocutors rapidly form ad hoc conventions.

> ***what are the hypotheses to be tested?***

(1) abstract concepts have more variable color associations. (2) individual uncertainty predicts partner agreement. (3) communication is initially harder for high-uncertainty concepts but improves rapidly.

---

# research design

> ***what is the methodological approach?***

two experiments: elicitation study of lexical priors, and an interactive dyadic communication task.

> ***what data is used?***

color-concept association ratings; dyadic communication accuracy across rounds.

> ***what are the specific methods and techniques?***

lexical prior elicitation; probabilistic modeling; interactive communication game; mixed-effects regression; RSA framework.

---

# results

> ***what are the key findings?***

abstract concepts have more variable/uncertain associations; uncertainty predicts partner misalignment; accuracy improves rapidly through ad hoc conventions.

> ***what do these findings say about the argument and hypotheses?***

lexical uncertainty is a meaningful, calibrated representation predicting coordination failure; the system is robust enough to recover.

---

# conclusion

> ***what is the overall conclusion?***

lexical uncertainty modulates communication difficulty and convention formation trajectory. people's representations are calibrated to predict misalignment and interaction rapidly bootstraps shared conventions.
